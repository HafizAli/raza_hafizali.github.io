About Us

Care for Women is a non-profit public policy organization based in Pakistan. It functions as a think tank, advocacy group and public outreach organization independent of government. CARE FOR WOMEN (CFW) seeks to promote knowledge-based policy making for intensification democratic institutions and building public stakes in human, empowering citizens, consumer and communities, national security dissertation by taking strategic action on public interest issues. It remains committed to investing in policies that promote fundamental rights, tolerance and pluralism.

Care for Women is a self-funded, not-for-profit organuzation registered in Pakistan. Inspired by a proactive commitment to the cause of peace, stability and human development, the Care for WOMEN aspires to make strategic interventions in Pakistan to support Peace, Stability and development. Care for WOMEN is working for various welfare, progressive and sustainable projects and studies in aspects of planning, research, development, management, finance, marketing, evaluation, strategies, policies, priorities, enactments, education and other concerned activities.

Care for Women (CFW) is established in the year 2009, and got registered in 20014, under Agencies Registration and Control Ordinance 1961 (XIVI of 1961) with the idea to empower and encourage Women, Person with Disabilities and young people for Decision making in all fields of life, Women Empowerment, Youth Development, Capacity Building, Poverty Alleviation, Rural Development, Emergency Relief, Public Library and Health Awareness Campaign Services in Central Punjab.

Publications

RURAL WOMEN ECONOMIC EMPOWERMENT THROUGH INFROAML SECTOR.

In this paper detail study was conducted on the empowerment of women, that how we can empower the women through the strengthening of Handicraft sector in rural areas. Empowerment means “to increase the spiritual, political, social and economic strength of individuals and communities” Women empowerment is the key element in improving the economic political and social conditions in Pakistan. The three pillars of women empowerment in Pakistan are: women economic participation and opportunity, women political empowerment, and women social empowerment which contain education attainment, health and other sectors. Promotion and protection of the handicraft sector the government can empower the women and can be able the women to earn fair income of their hard work.

GOVERNMENT STRATEGY FOR MADRASSAH, REFORM: EVALUATION, OPTIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS.

The terms ‘Koranic School’ and ‘Madrassah are often confused with each other by the Western critics. In the perception of the Western World, the Madrassahs in Pakistan are the breeding places for militant movements in the South Asian, and the Central Asian regions, as most of the operatives of the Taliban Government in Afghanistan were ‘ educated’ in this religious school system.
